# Research
- "Vunerable populations" may include neurodivergent identities in the context of working with Focus Bear. Challenges they may face in digital spaces include distractability, motivation and focus.
- Ethical considerations may include designing for different processing styles, inclusive and minimalist UX designs, easy-to-understand language, and clear explainations.
- As mentioned above, interactions and content can be made more accessible by aiming to reduce cognative load. This may include using predictable layouts and familar design language to rely on previous knowledge rather than overwhelming users with new information.
- Neurodivergent team members (such as myself) can be supported by using clear communication - explaining expectations and goals plainly and avoiding using implications to communicate. Flexability should also be implemented to accomodate differing working and processing styles.

# Reflection
- As a neurodivergent person myself, I feel my communication syle is quite inclusive already. However I continue to try to improve my approach, and am mindful of using clear and intentional communication.
- Overwhelming UX design or messy design langauge could make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive and cause users to disengage.
- I will ensure the language I use within software I develop is clear and easy to understand. I will also aim to lean on assumed knowledge and familar designs to communicate software's intentions.
- A guided tour of the application may make Focus Bear's tools easier to use, as it would relieve user's from having to break down the new information for themselves. This may be particularly helpful for Autistic users who struggle to breakdow and approach new concepts.
