# Research

## Risks of using a laptop without peripheral devices
- Neck and shoulder strain, due to the keyboard and screen being connected. Users must hunch over the screen for long periods of time and laptop screens do not allow for ergonomic adjustment
- Keyboard and trackpad use may cause discomfort in wrists and fingers
- Small laptop screens may cause eye strain and fatigue, as well as headaches

## Ergonomic equipment availible
- Ergonomic mouse and keyboard options such as southpaw keyboards
- Laptop stands
- Ergonomic deak chairs
- Foot rests
- Laptop stands

## Adjustment
- Desk and chair alignment should ensure user's feet sit flat on the floor and the back is straight with the head not bent down

## Daily habits
- Frequent stretch and movement breaks
- Periodically looking at objects at a distance can aid in preventing eye strain

# Reflection

## Equipment changes
My at-home workspace already includes a laptop stand, ergonomic mouse, and external keyboard and monitor. I make use of a standing desk to further reduce strain from prolonged sitting. However, when working out of the house I do not generally make use of any peripheral equipment, so I could endevour to bring a laptop stand or mouse with me when working away from home.

## Behavioural changes
I certainly could improve my work practices through behavioural changes, as I am currently very bad at taking breaks. I should endevour to take regular 5 minute movement breaks, and will use Focus Bear to remind me to do so.

## How?
I will set up Focus Bear reminders to take regular movement or stretch breaks to ensure I stay comfortable while working.

## One change made
I have added a microbreak habit in Focus Bear to remind me every 60 minutes to stretch and take a 2 minute movement break. This should reduce fatigue and strain while working.
