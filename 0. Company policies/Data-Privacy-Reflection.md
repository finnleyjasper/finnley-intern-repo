## Best Practices
- When handling client data, a risk-based approach is best use to safe guard confidential data. This means utilising encryption, strict access control, and adequate training within the compaby

- Only those who require access to data should be able to interact with it

## How to respond to a suspected data breach
- If a data breach is suspected, a four step approach may be used.
### Contain
    - Contain the breach by taking immediate action. Within my role, this may mean notifying my supervisor as soon as a breach is suspected. Unauthorised access should be stopped immidiately, which may mean shutting down affected systems, changing passwords and isolating compromised servers.
### Assess
    - Assemble a team for incident response. This may include IT security personnel, legal counsel, managament, and other relevent departments.
    - Assess the risk and evaluate the harm and extent of the attack - whether it affects internal personnel, clients, or financial systems.

### Notify
    - Any affected parties should be notified
    - Regulatory bodies should be contacted, as determined by law; possibly the police, also, depending on the event

### Review
    - The cause of the incient and any weaknesses in infrastructure should be reviewed and improved upon to prevent future issues
