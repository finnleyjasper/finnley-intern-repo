# What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?
Focus Bear's main differnce to the apps given as examples is that it combines many aspects of digital wellbeing into one application.

While these apps offer a range of features, you would ultimately need to use a number of them to achieve what Focus Bear offers - task management, "focus sessions", productivity tracking, habit support and distraction blocking.

Focus Bear also offers fantastic cross platform support, which is not present in many of these apps.

# If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?
I would choose Focus Bear over other apps due to its integration of all these features; it means I would not have to juggle using an app to track my productivity, and a wellbeing app to encourage me to stick to my habits.

I also work across a higher number of platforms than I believe most people do - an Android phone, Macbook for productivity, and iPad and PC for recreation - so having an app that can work across these devices is very appealing.

# What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?
More granular task-management. While Focus Bear does have a to-do list which has very good task prioritisation tools, compared to a product such as Notion, it does not offer much in the way of task management.

# Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?
I would love to see deeper task organisation, including "projects" you could assign tasks to. Additionally, I think being able to switch views of tasks to something like a Kanban board would be fantastic - particularly for the ADHD/Autistic market who are often overwhelmed by dense written information, and may appreciate a visual representation of their tasks and their progress (ie. being able to move tasks across the Kanban board)
