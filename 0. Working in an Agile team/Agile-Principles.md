# Reflection
- Some noteable benefits of an Agile workflow include the flexibility and adaptability of the approach. Continuously iterating on work and checking in with teammates means the product being produced is more likely to fit company/client needs, including if those needs change over the lifetime of the project
- Agile also emphasises communication with frequent sharing of achievements and difficulties. This increases effective teamwork and allows for people to collaborate effectively

- Some challenges of the Agile workflow may include a potential lack of long-term planning, do to the approach's focus on quick, continuous improvements
- Agile may also present issues if the team is not effective with communication, as the workflow relies heavily on these skills
- Additionally, Agile requires users to effectively break down and estimate the importance and time requirement of tasks. If the team is not proficient in this, that may cause productivity issues and many issues blocking task progression

- Agile principles that will help me improve in my role and deliver on my responsibilities include the emphasis on frequent refleciton, and collaboration. I believe consistently looking back on work I deliver and taking on feedback will help me to improve, particularly as I am not overly familiar with backend development. For the same reason, I expect Agile's key principle of consistent communciation and check-ins will help me fulfill my responsibilities, where I can ask questions and improve my understanding through conversation with my colleagues

# Tasks
- Scrum differs from Kanban by emphasising short "sprints" where deliverables are worked on quickly, then delivered. Tasks are assigned to members for completion within these key periods, devliered, then new tasks are allocated. This approach works best for prototyping or for projects with a clear development goal. On the other hand, Kanban emphasises an iterative and steady workflow, where small tasks are assigned to individuals and worked on piece-by-piece, moving from the backlog across stages before being completed. This approach works best for constant and incremental improvements and developments, such as improving an already-existing product or fixing bugs - for situations where there is not a concrete deliverable or larger product to produce

- As discussed above, I believe Agile's emphasis on frequent reflection and collaborative communication are two principles that will be particularly usful to me throughout my role
